Frequently Asked Questions.

Delegated Administrative Privileges (DAP) and Lighthouse permissions for Enterprise Agreement and Web Direct customers.

As an authorised Microsoft Direct Bill Partner, and an Azure Expert MSP, Macquarie Cloud Services require access to your Microsoft services in order to provide managed services for your business or organisation.

On this page you will find answers to FAQs regarding the access we require for Managed Azure services, and the measures we take to protect that access.

What am I accepting?

As part of on boarding you to a managed service, we request that you accept two relationship requests that enable us to manage your subscriptions. You will be asked to approve:

  1. a Lighthouse Azure Marketplace offer that provides delegated administrative privileges on the Azure subscriptions billed by Microsoft. (Example: subscription level roles for an Enterprise Agreement or a Microsoft Online Services offer)
  2. a Granular Delegated Administrative Privileges (GDAP) request that effectively gives us Azure Active Directory rights in your tenant for a combination of roles. (Tenant level roles)
    Once accepted, Macquarie Cloud Services will be provided with granular and time-bound access to the set of roles in-line with our managed services scope of works.

What is the Azure Marketplace Managed Services offer?

The Azure subscription permissions we require when managing subscriptions that are billed directly by Microsoft are delivered through Lighthouse. The roles and groups are injected into your tenancy through the acceptance of a Managed Services offer published in the Azure Marketplace. Lighthouse does not have Azure AD rights, or Data Actions rights, and the highest Azure role possible is Contributor.

Why do you need Azure subscription privileges?

We require permissions at the Azure subscription level in order for:

  • HMC to lodge support tickets with Microsoft,
  • Solution Engineers to action Moves, Adds and Changes requests,
  • Architects to manage and maintain automation, RBAC and management architecture
  • Systems to ingest inventory and the CMDB
  • HMC to monitor, alert, and action ticket incidents
  • PCTOs to design, analyse, and optimise usage
  • Service Delivery Managers to assist with costs and inventory requests.

The roles and permissions may be viewed by going to the subscription, access control, role assignments. You may review this either asking a list of the permissions beforehand or at the time of acceptance of the Azure Marketplace offer.

How do I accept the Marketplace offer?

  • Please log into to the Azure marketplace with the credentials of the Owner of the subscription.
  • Click on the icon “Get it now”.
  • You will be taken to the Azure portal to accept the offer, picking the Managed or Essentials plan as applicable.
  • Don’t forget to delegate the subscriptions to the accepted offer.
  • Check that we are now listed as your service provider by going to the Service Provider blade.

What is the GDAP relationship request?

Under the GDAP program, we nominate granular roles and privileges that provide just enough permissions in order to execute the managed services. These roles are injected into your tenancy, which means the groups, and our users do not exist in your Azure Active Directory tenancy. These privileges are not permanent and are only activated by us when required.

Based on the directory roles assigned, members of our groups can manage your Azure services using our partner credentials and delegated administrative privileges.

Why do you need Azure Active Directory privileges?

Managed Azure
The collection of AAD roles that we request provides access to Azure Active Directory, which is the master identity store for services in Microsoft’s cloud. Our Managed Azure identity and access management services currently use the roles to:

  • Automate the central logging of Azure AD logs for audit purposes.
  • Automate systems ingestion of inventory, configuration management, and ticketing.
  • Monitor the use of selected privileged accounts.
  • Create and manage service principals.
  • Assist in Conditional Access and Multi-Factor Authentication design and implementation.
  • Enable subscriptions to tenant mapping into billing, configuration and ticket management systems
The GDAP roles below reflect the Azure Managed services proposal made to you. A GDAP link will be sent to you to review and accept the Azure Active Directory relationship requests, that lasts for 730 days. A user with Global Admin permission is required to accept the relationship.: No sort applied, activate to apply an ascending sort

Azure AD roles:

  1. Cloud application administrator: Can create and manage all aspects of app registrations and enterprise apps except App Proxy.
  2. Conditional access administrator: Can manage conditional access capabilities.
  3. License administrator: Ability to assign, remove and update license assignments.
  4. Privileged role administrator: Can manage role assignments in Azure AD, and all aspects of Privileged Identity Management.
  5. Billing administrator: Can perform common billing related tasks like updating payment information.
  6. Service support administrator: Can read service health information and manage support tickets.
  7. Directory readers: Can read basic directory information. Commonly used to grant directory read access to applications and guests.
  8. Security administrator: Can read security information and reports and manage configuration in Azure AD and Office 365.
  9. Security operator: Can create and manage security events
  10. User Administrator: Can manage all aspects of users and groups, including resetting passwords for limited admins.

Note: GDAP acceptance does not create a commercial “rebilling” relationship or impact Azure subscription roles.

 

How do I accept the relationship request?

  1. Sign into Microsoft 365 Admin Center as Global Admin.
  2. Paste the GDAP link into your browser.
  3. From the Approve partner roles page that opens in the Microsoft 365 admin Center, please select Approve all.
  4. You will receive an email confirmation on the delegated approval.
You may choose not to grant us these Azure Active Directory roles, however, this would lengthen the on-boarding
process (as we have to request your action and remediation to gather domains, access, logs, etc.) and impact
on-going management, as explained above. You may choose:

1. A minimal set of roles (Cloud Application Administrator, Directory Readers, License Administrator and Service Support Administrator). This will enable ticketing,
however, we may not be able to fulfil the complete scope of the designed Managed Services as relevant
to Azure Active Directory. 
2. A custom set of roles. Please engage with your Macquarie Cloud Services account manager in order to
further describe your requirements.

What security measures are in place?

  • Role Based Access Control. Engineers are members of the groups that have the Azure subscription and AAD privileged rights. Account Executives and Service Delivery Managers have commercial permissions. Service Desk Tier 1 engineers may log support tickets on your behalf.
  • Just-in-Time access. Macquarie Cloud Services uses Privileged Identity Management to protect customers. Roles and privileges are not permanent, but are activated on demand, whenever Tier 1/2 engineers require to execute actions, such as provisioning or troubleshooting. These automatically expire after the set period of time finishes. Actions are logged and audited for anomalies. Customers are able to monitor service provider activity by going to Monitor service provider activity – Azure Lighthouse | Microsoft Docs.
  • Multi-Factor Authentication. Macquarie identities and access measures are protected through MFA.
  • Just-enough privileges. Tier 1 help desk and non-technical staff are members in groups that have less privileges delegated, based on the least privileges principle.

What happens after acceptance of the relationships?

Once accepted, Macquarie Cloud Services will be provided with granular and time-bound access to the set of roles nominated.

Updating Lighthouse Permissions

From time to time, the permissions as published in the Marketplace offer change, due to solution and technology requirements. You can update the service provider offer by:

  1. Finding our offer at Service providers – Microsoft Azure
  2. Updating the offer by clicking the “update” icon. View and manage service providers – Azure Lighthouse | Microsoft Docs.

if you wish to remove our permissions, an Owner can remove the entire offer or remove the delegations for that offer. Remove access to a delegation – Azure Lighthouse | Microsoft Docs

 

Updating GDAP permissions

Renew

You’ll receive email reminders when the GDAP relationship expiration date is near (30 days, 7 days, and 1 day from the expiration date), and we will send you a new GDAP relationship request. If nothing is done, access will automatically expire once the existing relationship duration is reached, and you’ll receive an email confirmation. We will no longer have access to your tenant, and our users can no longer administer services on your behalf.

Change

  • When you move a subscription from an Essentials to a Managed plan, please accept the Managed subscription GDAP request.
  • When you move a subscription from a Managed plan to an Essentials plan,

Audit

You can track our activity by reviewing the AAD sign-in logs.

End

You may terminate a GDAP relationship by signing in as a Global Admin to the Microsoft 365 Admin Center, going to Partner relationships and selecting and removing the relevant relationship.
